Uncle Thomas

Borrowing the name of the doubting disciple, the film calls out the cynic in all of us and reframes success as something enabled by belief.

The brief

Start a project

Uncle Thomas is a campaign that confronts doubt. Named after the biblical figure who refused to believe without proof, the film uses humour and cultural familiarity to challenge the Nigerian audience's default cynicism about ambition and success.

The idea was simple: everyone knows an Uncle Thomas — the voice that says it cannot be done, the relative who dismisses your plans, the colleague who reminds you of every risk. The campaign reframes that doubt as something to push past, not give in to.

The Problem

Doubt was louder than ambition

In Nigerian culture, cynicism around success is deeply embedded. People with ambition are often met with discouragement from the very people closest to them. The client needed to position itself on the side of belief.

The Insight

Everyone knows an Uncle Thomas

The doubting relative is a universal Nigerian figure. Not a villain, just someone who has seen too many plans fail and projects it onto everyone else. That familiarity made the insight instantly recognisable.

The Idea

Name the doubt to defeat it

By giving doubt a face and a name — Uncle Thomas — we made it something people could laugh at, call out and move past. The film turned cynicism into a character and belief into the hero.
